Subscription UX prototypes

Self-contained prototypes (mock data, no backend) you can click through to validate. Two sets: simplifying subscription creation, and adding filtering to the subscriptions grid. Open each, then tell me which to ship.

Subscription creation — 4 options

Option 1

Segmented toggle

A compact User / Group switch at the top with one inline form below. Everything stays on a single, small screen — no separate panels.

 Option 2

Progressive disclosure

One simple form defaulting to a personal subscription. A “Share with a group” switch reveals the group picker only when needed.

 Option 3

Dropdown selector

The most minimal: a type dropdown + name field + Subscribe button in one tidy row. The group field appears only for group subscriptions.

 Option 4

Compact icon cards

The familiar cards, but smaller, with icons, and they expand inline (accordion) instead of replacing the whole view.

Grid filtering — 4 options

Filter 1

Inline filter toolbar

A compact toolbar above the grid: name search plus Type, Product and State dropdowns, a live result count and one-click Clear. Filters apply instantly and combine.

 Filter 2

Search + quick-filter chips

A search box plus one-click toggle chips for the common facets (Type, State) and a small Product dropdown. Optimised for the most frequent filters.

 Filter 3

Per-column header filters

Excel-style filter controls embedded in a second header row, right under each column title. Filtering lives where the data is, with a pinned header.

 Filter 4

Collapsible filter drawer

A search box plus a “Filters” button with an active-count badge that opens a side drawer of grouped facets with live counts, Apply/Clear and removable active-filter chips.
